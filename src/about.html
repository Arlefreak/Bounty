At a first glance, Super Bounty Hunter X is a simple game about social interactions with other people. It's what we think should be the bare essentials to any modern mobile game: it's fun, it creates and handles data transfers extremely well, and it capitalizes on the relationship between players, time, and places to create a novel social experience. Also, it's made with some of the best technology available.

Here's some of the details:

We use Elixir to handle the back end. With it, we can work data concurrency with ease and make full use of AT&T's M2X service, which captures the data from user cellphones as individual devices - each of which reports a rich data history in the most efficient manner possible (and it's ARO-tested).

For databases, all of our users' interaction history is stored in Neo4J - a great graph database. This shortens query response times and makes finding the little insights a lot faster. It also just makes sense in a game where everything and everyone is connected through events.

Also, we have built a native Android app and compatible HTML 5 website (Backbone.js), all with custom graphics and art by our own design team. We've thrown together an Avatar Creator uses custom scripts and ImageMagick to create random and unique avatars based on the player's entered name.
